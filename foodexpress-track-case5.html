üí° AI Mentor: Crisis Management

Recommended Priority: Communicate with Partners & Customers First

Why? In delivery partner strike situations, follow this order:

1. Communicate quickly - Immediately notify customers about delays and offer alternatives (prevents order cancellations & complaints)
2. Engage delivery partners - Understand concerns, offer temporary incentives, negotiate resolution (addresses root cause)
3. Activate backup plan - Partner with other delivery fleets or implement emergency measures (ensures business continuity)

üìö Framework: This follows the Stakeholder Crisis Management Matrix: Communicate ‚Üí Negotiate ‚Üí Activate Contingency. In multi-stakeholder crises, transparency to all parties prevents escalation.

üè¢ Real Example: When Swiggy faced delivery partner strikes in Bangalore (2020), they immediately sent in-app notifications to 50,000 customers, offered 30% refunds, then negotiated with partners (improved incentives within 4 hours). They activated backup fleet partnerships simultaneously. 85% of customers appreciated transparency and stayed loyal.

üìñ Storytelling Practice

Scenario: The crisis is fixed (order routing back up). Now you need to send an email to all 2,500 affected restaurant partners explaining what happened.

How would you communicate this crisis to restaurants? (Write 2-3 sentences)

‚ùå Weak Communication (Defensive/Technical):

"We experienced a server routing failure that impacted order processing for 90 minutes. Our engineering team resolved the issue. We apologize for any inconvenience caused."

Why weak: Generic, defensive, no empathy. "Inconvenience" minimizes real business impact. No transparency about what they'll do differently.

‚úÖ Strong Communication (Honest + Empathetic):

"Today, 3,200 orders were stuck for 90 minutes because our routing system failed. We know this hurt your business - missed lunch rush, wasted food prep, angry customers calling you. We fixed it in 45 minutes and added redundant routing so it won't happen again. If you lost revenue, email us - we're reviewing compensation case-by-case. You deserve better from FoodExpress."

Why strong: Specific impact (3,200 orders), acknowledges restaurant pain ("missed lunch rush, wasted food prep"), owns it ("you deserve better"), concrete action ("redundant routing"), offers remedy ("reviewing compensation"). Builds trust through honesty.

üí° Crisis Communication Formula:
Specific Impact (what broke) + Empathy (acknowledge pain) + Ownership (we failed you) + Prevention (won't happen again) + Remedy (how we make it right) = Trust restored

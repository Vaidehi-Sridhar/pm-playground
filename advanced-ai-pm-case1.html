Build vs Buy AI

Should we build a custom LLM, use Claude API, or take a hybrid approach?

üìå The Challenge

Your company's customer support platform processes 10,000 AI-assisted requests monthly. Currently, 40% requires manual review because your rule-based system lacks context understanding.

Engineering wants to integrate LLMs. You have three options:

Option A: Build Custom LLM

Upfront Cost: $800K (training + infrastructure)
Monthly Cost: $20K (hosting + maintenance)
18-Month Total: $1.16M
Time to Production: 18 months

Pros: Full control, no vendor dependency, data stays internal, can fine-tune for specific use cases
Cons: Long timeline, requires ML expertise, ongoing maintenance burden, uncertain quality

Option B: Use Claude API

Upfront Cost: $50K (integration)
Monthly Cost: $25K (API usage at scale)
18-Month Total: $500K
Time to Production: 6 months

Pros: Fast deployment, maintained by Anthropic, proven quality, scales automatically
Cons: Vendor lock-in, usage costs scale with volume, data leaves your infrastructure, rate limits

Option C: Hybrid Approach

Upfront Cost: $300K (infrastructure + integration)
Monthly Cost: $35K (hosting + API)
18-Month Total: $930K
Time to Production: 12 months

Pros: Balanced approach, use Claude for complex cases, smaller model for simple ones, gradual migration path
Cons: Complexity of managing two systems, more integration work, higher operational overhead

üéì Key Learnings: Build vs Buy Decisions

  • Build vs Buy is rarely binary: Hybrid approaches often balance innovation speed with control
  • Hidden costs matter: Custom LLMs require ML expertise, maintenance, monitoring - not just upfront training
  • Time-to-market has value: 6 months vs 18 months means 12 months of manual work continues (40% * 10K * 12 = 48K manual reviews)
  • Vendor lock-in isn't always bad: If vendor provides value and you can't build better in-house, dependency is acceptable
  • Stakeholder alignment is critical: CFO cares about total cost, CTO cares about scalability - address both explicitly
  • Define success metrics upfront: How will you know in 6 months if this was the right call?

üìå The Challenge

Your company is a B2B SaaS platform ($12M ARR, 120 employees). Growth is slowing (15% YoY). 5 of 7 competitors launched AI features. Sales is losing deals: "Do you have an AI copilot?"

CEO Emily wants a 12-month AI roadmap for the board meeting in 2 weeks. You need to define: vision, competitive positioning, quarterly OKRs, resource allocation, and success metrics. This is a strategic document, not a feature list.

Market Research Findings

What Customers Actually Want (Survey of 200 users):
  • Better search (78% want this)
  • Automated report generation (64%)
  • Smart recommendations (52%)
  • Conversational chatbot (31% - least requested!)
What Competitors Are Building:
  • All 5 competitors: AI chatbot / copilot
  • 3 competitors: Automated insights
  • 1 competitor: AI-powered search (launched 2 months ago, getting great reviews)
What Sales Says:

"Prospects check off 'AI copilot' on RFPs. If we don't have it, we're out. But existing customers don't use competitors' chatbots much - they say search is still broken."

Resource Constraints

AI Budget (next 12 months): $2M
Engineering Capacity: 25 engineers (5 ML, 20 full-stack)
Current AI Spend: $15K/month (existing basic ML)
Board Expectation: Ship something in Q1, scale in Q2-Q4

Strategic Directions to Consider

Direction A: "AI-First Platform" (Aggressive)

Build AI into every product surface. Copilot, search, reports, recommendations - ship 6-8 AI features in 12 months. High investment, high visibility.

Direction B: "Solve One Problem Exceptionally" (Focused)

Pick ONE high-impact area (e.g., AI-powered search) and make it world-class. Ignore copilot/chatbot hype. Differentiate on what customers actually need.

Direction C: "Fast Follow + One Innovation" (Balanced)

Ship basic copilot in Q1 (table stakes for sales), then invest Q2-Q4 in differentiated AI feature (search or insights). Balanced risk.

12-Month AI Product Strategy & Roadmap

üìù Instructions

Write an 800+ word strategic roadmap document. Your document should include:

  • Executive Summary: Your AI vision in 3-4 sentences
  • Market Analysis: What customers want vs what competitors are building
  • Strategic Direction: Which of the 3 directions (A/B/C) and comprehensive reasoning
  • Competitive Positioning: How will we differentiate in a crowded AI market?
  • 12-Month Roadmap: Quarterly OKRs and milestones (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)
  • Resource Allocation: How you'll spend $2M budget across 4 quarters
  • Success Metrics: KPIs to measure if AI strategy is working (business + product metrics)
  • Risks & Mitigation: What could derail this strategy? How will you prevent it?
0 / 800 words minimum

üí° Writing Tips

  • Customers want search (78%), competitors are building chatbots. This gap is your opportunity to differentiate.
  • Sales says "we need copilot checkbox for RFPs" - you can ship basic copilot in Q1 for sales, then focus on differentiation Q2-Q4.
  • OKRs must be measurable: "Ship AI copilot" is a milestone, not an OKR. "Achieve 30% copilot adoption among daily active users" is an OKR.
  • Budget allocation shows prioritization: If you spend 60% on search and 20% on copilot, that signals search is your differentiation bet.
  • Address the "why us?" question: If 5 competitors already have AI, why will customers choose our AI? Need a differentiation story.
  • Risks should be specific: "AI hallucinations damage trust" not "AI might not work". Then show mitigation: "Implement human-in-loop for high-stakes answers".

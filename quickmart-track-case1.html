Product Sense

Interview merchants before deciding what to build

üìß The Request

A merchant emailed asking for Voice Search integration.

Before deciding whether to build it, interview the merchant to understand their actual problem and needs.

‚è∞ REALITY CHECK

In real PM work, this takes 1-2 weeks because:

  • 8-12 customer interviews (60-90 min each) to validate the problem
  • Survey 100+ shoppers to quantify pain point frequency
  • Competitive analysis of Blinkit, Zepto features
  • Supply chain review for feature feasibility

Real example: When Blinkit built voice search (2021), they interviewed 35+ customers doing household chores over 2 weeks before prototyping.

üìñ Storytelling Practice

Scenario: You need to pitch voice ordering to your engineering lead Priya. She's skeptical because the team is already building cart recommendations and dark mode.

How would you tell this story to convince her? (Write 2-3 sentences)

‚ùå Weak Story (Data Dump):

"Voice ordering will reduce cart abandonment by 18% for busy parents. We have 45,000 customers who order 3+ times per week. The business impact is ‚Çπ2.5Cr annual GMV from reduced abandonment."

Why weak: Just numbers. No human story. Engineering doesn't feel the customer pain. Sounds like a business request, not a customer problem.

‚úÖ Strong Story (Narrative):

"Riya is a working mom with 2 kids. She orders groceries 4 times a week - milk, vegetables, snacks. Right now at 7 PM while cooking dinner, she realizes she's out of milk. She has wet hands and a baby on her hip, trying to type 'milk, bread, eggs' into our app. She abandons 30% of her carts because typing is too frustrating. She told me: 'If I could just speak my order while cooking, I'd never use Blinkit again.' Can we build voice ordering for busy parents like Riya?"

Why strong: "Riya" (character), "wet hands, baby on hip" (specific problem), "while cooking dinner" (context), "abandons 30%" (stakes), "never use Blinkit" (retention), "Can we build...?" (collaboration). Engineering feels Riya's pain and wants to help her.

üí° Storytelling Formula:
Character (Riya) + Problem (typing while multitasking) + Stakes (30% abandonment, might leave) + Solution (voice ordering) + Scale (45,000 busy parents) = Compelling narrative
